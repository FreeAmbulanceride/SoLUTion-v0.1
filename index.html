Why 60/30/10?

Have you ever seen a photograph, a set design, or a living room that just felt right? This effortless harmony isn’t magic—it’s often the result of a silent, centuries-old agreement on how the human eye processes color. That agreement is the 60:30:10 ratio, the unsung hero of visual balance and the principle at the heart of soLUTion 60:30:10.

The Architecture of Visual Harmony

At its core, 60:30:10 is an elegant framework for assigning roles to color. It ensures that every element on your canvas—be it a digital screen or a physical room—is working in concert, not shouting over one another. It's a guideline, not a rigid law, but one that has stood the test of time across every visual discipline.

  • 60% Primary: Your anchor, the dominant color that grounds the whole scene.
  • 30% Secondary: The supporting rhythm, adding texture, contrast, and depth.
  • 10% Accent: The spark of energy that directs attention and adds focus.

This proportion succeeds because it taps into our preference for order over chaos. It calms a scene by giving the eye a clear path to follow, making balance feel intentional, even to non-experts.

From Cinema Frames to Living Rooms

In Interior Design, the ratio defines a room’s flow: a neutral wall and large sofa (60%), supporting textures or wood tones (30%), and bold cushions or art (10%). Ignore it and the space feels agitated with competing colors.

Photographers and filmmakers rely on it to guide attention: a muted environment (60%), complementary wardrobe or props (30%), and one striking accent (10%). That accent acts as a visual magnet.

In Branding, the hero product sits on a dominant background (60%), props fill in 30%, and a metallic highlight or sharp shadow (10%) keeps the hero popping.

Why a Live Guide Matters

You already know when a shot is off, but seeing the math is hard when you’re close to your work. soLUTion 60:30:10 removes guesswork by scoring your composition live.

The real-time feedback trains your intuition. As you move props or shift the camera, you see the score rise and fall. Hitting 100% triggers the subtle chime, making strong composition feel like a gentle challenge instead of a chore.
